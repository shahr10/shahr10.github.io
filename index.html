About

Research + engineering focus, positioned for both PhD applications and systems/GNC roles.

What I work on

I’m a graduate researcher in Aerospace Engineering at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University, affiliated with the Explainable Intelligence for Space (XD) Lab. My work spans cislunar orbital dynamics (CR3BP), quantum key distribution (QKD) performance analysis, and safe autonomy (reachability + learning).

I care about problems that require end-to-end systems thinking: realistic constraints, measurable metrics, and conclusions that transfer to mission design.

Technical interests

  • Astrodynamics: CR3BP propagation, cislunar mission/constellation design, access/coverage analysis
  • GNC: nonlinear dynamics, guidance laws, trajectory optimization, estimation & validation
  • Safe autonomy: Hamilton–Jacobi reachability, robust control, learning-enabled safety
  • Quantum systems: QKD link budgets, key availability constraints, network-level performance
  • Learning: PINNs, surrogate modeling, federated learning under comms constraints
